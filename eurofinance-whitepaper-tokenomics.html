Euro Finance (WERC) – Short Whitepaper

Version: 1.0
Last update: December 2025

1. Overview

Euro Finance (ticker: WERC) is a multi-chain euro-themed DeFi asset deployed on BNB Smart Chain (BSC) and Arbitrum (with TRON and other networks planned).

WERC is not a stablecoin, is not pegged 1:1 to EUR, and is not affiliated with Circle or Euro Coin (EURC). The price of WERC is market-driven and may deviate significantly from 1 EUR.

The goal of the project is to provide a simple, transparent and flexible on-chain euro-themed asset that can be used in decentralized finance (DeFi), payments, liquidity provision and ecosystem utilities.

This short whitepaper describes the basic concept of the project, key principles, contracts and high-level tokenomics. It is intentionally compact and may be extended with more detailed technical documentation later.

6. Utility and Integrations

6.1. Utility

  • DeFi integrations: WERC can be used in liquidity pools, yield farming, lending/borrowing protocols and other DeFi platforms where it is integrated.
  • Cross-chain flows: as a multi-chain asset, WERC can be bridged or migrated between networks (for example, between BSC and Arbitrum) using official bridges or migration mechanisms announced by the project.
  • Payments and settlements: where supported, WERC can be used for payments, B2B settlements and internal accounting inside the ecosystem.
  • Incentive layer: WERC may be used for staking, loyalty programs and other incentive mechanisms decided by the Euro Finance team.

7. Governance & Transparency

The Euro Finance project follows several core principles:

  1. Open source & audits:
    • Smart contract code is publicly available and can be independently reviewed.
    • Security audits and verifications from third-party auditors are published via official channels.
  2. Clear communication:
    • Changes to tokenomics, supply or core protocol parameters are announced in advance via the official website and social channels.
  3. Official information channels:
    • Official website and documentation pages.
    • Verified social media channels (Telegram, X/Twitter, etc.).
    • Listings and profiles on CoinMarketCap and other aggregators, once approved.
  4. Locked tokens transparency:
    • Any locked or vested allocations are published with on-chain proof (time-lock contracts, vesting contracts) and links on BscScan / Arbiscan / TRON explorers.
